Spaghetti Carbonara

Posted by: Maria G. March 10, 2025
30 Minutes
2 Servings
Easy Difficulty

About This Recipe

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish that is rich, creamy, and incredibly satisfying. Made with just a few simple ingredients, the key to a great carbonara is using the pasta's cooking water to create a silky sauce from the eggs and cheese. No cream is used in an authentic carbonara!

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Preparation Steps

  • 1
    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package directions until al dente. Before draining, save about 1 cup of pasta water.
  • 2
    While the pasta cooks, fry the pancetta in a large pan over medium heat until crispy. Add the garlic cloves and cook for 1 minute, then remove the garlic.
  • 3
    In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolk, Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan. Season with black pepper.
  • 4
    Add the drained pasta to the pan with the pancetta (heat turned off). Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing constantly.
  • 5
    Add pasta water a little at a time and keep tossing until you get a creamy sauce. The residual heat will cook the eggs slowly without scrambling them.
  • 6
    Serve immediately with extra black pepper and grated cheese on top.
